# Customer Workspace Handbook

Version: 1.0  
Audience: Customer teams using the workspace  
Purpose: Help customers understand the product and use it confidently in day-to-day work

## Welcome

This handbook explains the workspace inside Messenger MVP in simple language. It is written for customers, not engineers.

The workspace brings communication, approvals, finance, warehouse work, tasks, projects, and analytics into one operating area. Instead of using separate tools for every part of the business, your team can work from one place.

## What the workspace helps you do

The workspace is designed to help a business:

- communicate with the team inside the same place where work happens
- review approvals quickly
- manage invoices and expenses
- monitor stock and purchase orders
- track tasks and projects
- see what needs attention through analytics

If you need one sentence to describe the product to a customer, use this:

> This workspace gives your team one place to communicate, review approvals, manage finance and warehouse work, track tasks, and see business pressure in real time.

## Who should read this handbook

This handbook is useful for:

- workspace owners
- managers
- team members
- finance users
- warehouse users

If someone is setting up workspaces across multiple customers, they should also read the platform owner guide.

## The two levels of access

There are two levels of access in the product.

### Platform owner

Platform owner is the app-level administrator. Platform owner can:

- create customer workspaces
- add members to customer workspaces
- add another platform owner
- change purchased modules
- change finance permissions
- disable a workspace
- delete a workspace
- delete app users when needed

Platform owner is not the same as workspace owner.

### Workspace roles

Workspace roles control what a person can do inside one workspace.

#### Workspace owner

Workspace owner is the main owner of one customer workspace. They can:

- manage team members in that workspace
- assign roles and access
- manage workspace settings
- use the modules assigned to them

#### Manager

Manager can run day-to-day operations and manage normal members. Manager is below workspace owner and cannot manage ownership-level access.

#### Member

Member is the base workspace role. What the member can actually use depends on:

- which modules are enabled
- which finance permissions are enabled

## Modules

Modules control the major product areas a person can access.

### Finance

Finance includes:

- invoices
- expenses
- approvals
- finance activity
- finance analytics
- accounting-related screens for the right users

### Warehouse

Warehouse includes:

- products
- low stock alerts
- purchase orders
- stock movement
- warehouse activity

If a user does not have a module enabled, that area may be hidden.

## Finance permissions

Finance permissions are separate from workspace role.

### Viewer

Viewer can read finance information and open read-only dashboards.

### Approver

Approver can review and approve or reject finance items waiting for approval.

### Finance staff

Finance staff can work on daily finance operations such as:

- creating and updating invoices
- creating and updating expenses
- working with payment matching
- handling finance workflows

### Accountant

Accountant can use deeper accounting views and reporting.

## What a normal customer will see

Depending on access, a customer may see:

- Inbox
- Overview / Analytics
- Approvals
- Finance
- Warehouse
- Tasks
- Projects
- Workspace settings

If something is missing, it usually means one of these is not enabled:

- workspace role
- module access
- finance permission
- workspace access itself

## First login checklist

When a customer starts using a workspace for the first time:

1. Confirm the right people are already in the workspace.
2. Check who should be workspace owner, manager, or member.
3. Confirm which users need Finance and which need Warehouse.
4. Confirm who needs Viewer, Approver, Finance staff, or Accountant permissions.
5. Open Inbox, Analytics, and the main modules together once so the team understands the layout.

## Navigation guide

The main navigation may include these sections:

### Inbox

Inbox is where active workspace conversations appear. It is used for:

- team messaging
- bot threads
- the shared workspace group conversation

### Overview / Analytics

Overview gives a top summary of the workspace. It helps answer:

- what is overdue?
- what needs approval?
- what is happening in finance?
- what is happening in warehouse?
- what needs attention today?

### Users

Users is where owners and managers can review team access, invite members, and adjust permissions.

### Finances

Finance is where finance workflows happen, including invoices, expenses, reports, and accounting-related views.

### Warehouse

Warehouse is where products, stock alerts, and purchase orders are managed.

### Task Manager

Tasks helps the team track work that is assigned, overdue, or due today.

### Project Management

Projects helps the team track larger initiatives, milestones, and team progress.

## Inbox and conversations

The workspace supports:

- direct workspace conversations between members
- workspace bot conversations such as FinanceBot and WareBot
- a shared workspace group conversation

### What appears in Inbox

Inbox shows active conversations. That means conversations with real activity or valid workspace threads.

### Workspace group conversation

The shared group conversation is for team-wide updates inside the workspace. A good use case is:

- daily updates
- team announcements
- quick coordination

### Bots

Bots are workspace helpers connected to specific modules.

- FinanceBot helps surface finance-related activity
- WareBot helps surface warehouse-related activity

In a real workspace, bot content should reflect real workspace data rather than demo content.

## Analytics in customer language

Analytics is your business snapshot. It gives decision-makers a quick answer to:

- what needs action right now?
- what is overdue?
- what is waiting for approval?
- what pressure is building in finance?
- what pressure is building in warehouse?

A simple customer explanation is:

> Analytics shows the health of your workspace at a glance. It highlights overdue work, approvals, inventory pressure, finance movement, and other items that need action.

## What each analytics area means

### Overview

Overview is the top summary. It usually shows:

- urgent issues
- overdue work
- approvals
- pressure across the workspace

### Finance analytics

Finance analytics may show:

- pending approvals
- overdue invoices
- paid and unpaid amounts
- expense activity
- cash movement

### Warehouse analytics

Warehouse analytics may show:

- low stock items
- purchase order progress
- warehouse movement
- stock pressure

### Tasks and projects

These show:

- overdue tasks
- due-today tasks
- project progress
- work that needs follow-up

## Daily workflow examples

### Example 1: Review approvals

1. Open Analytics or Finance.
2. Check items waiting for approval.
3. Open the item.
4. Approve or reject it.

### Example 2: Review invoices and expenses

1. Open Finance.
2. Review invoices, expenses, customers, and payments.
3. Follow the status from draft or review into approval and payment matching.

### Example 3: Check stock pressure

1. Open Warehouse.
2. Review low-stock items.
3. Create or follow purchase orders.

### Example 4: Track team work

1. Open Tasks.
2. Check overdue work and due-today work.
3. Reassign, update, or complete items.

### Example 5: Follow project progress

1. Open Projects.
2. Review project status and progress.
3. Update milestones and team work as needed.

### Finance operator

### Warehouse operator

- Modules: Warehouse

## Common questions

### Why can’t I see Finance?

Check whether:

- the Finance module is enabled
- the person has the right finance permission
- the person still has workspace access

### Why can’t I see Warehouse?

Check whether the Warehouse module is enabled for that person.

### Why can I use the workspace but not manage people?

That usually means the user is a Member instead of a Workspace owner or Manager.

### Why can the platform owner do more than the workspace owner?

Because platform owner manages the app across all workspaces, while workspace owner only manages one workspace.

### What happens if someone is removed from a workspace?

They should lose access to that workspace and should no longer continue working inside it.

## Troubleshooting

### I refreshed and something disappeared for a moment

Try refreshing once more. If the problem continues, ask your admin to confirm that:

- your workspace is active
- your workspace access is still enabled
- your selected workspace is correct

### A section is empty

Possible reasons:

- no records exist yet
- the module is not enabled
- your permission level is read-only

### I cannot approve something

Check whether you have:

- Approver permission
- or a higher finance role that includes approval authority

### I cannot manage member access

Check whether you are:

- workspace owner
- manager with the required access
- or platform owner

## Support checklist for customer admins

If a customer says “I can’t use the workspace properly,” check these in order:

1. Are they in the correct workspace?
2. Is their workspace access enabled?
3. Is their base workspace role correct?
4. Are the right modules enabled?
5. Are the right finance permissions enabled?

## Glossary

### Workspace

The business operating area inside the app.

### Module

A major feature area such as Finance or Warehouse.

### Finance permission

An access level inside Finance, such as Viewer or Accountant.

### Approval

A business item waiting for someone with approval authority to review it.

### Payment matching

Matching a payment or bank movement to the related finance record.

## Final summary

This product works best when roles are simple and intentional:

- one workspace owner
- one or more managers
- members with only the modules and permissions they actually need

If customers follow that structure, the workspace becomes much easier to understand and much easier to run.
