# Workspace Customer Guide

This guide is for customers using the workspace inside Messenger MVP. It explains what the workspace does, what each role means, and how to use the main parts of the product without technical language.

## What the workspace is

The workspace is your company operating area inside the app. It brings together:

- team conversations
- approvals
- finance work
- warehouse work
- tasks and projects
- notifications
- analytics

The purpose is simple: your team can see what needs attention and act from one place.

## The two levels of access

There are two different layers of access in the product.

### Platform owner

Platform owner is the app-level administrator. Platform owner can:

- create customer workspaces
- add customers to workspaces
- change purchased modules
- change finance permissions
- disable or delete a workspace

Platform owner is not the same as a customer workspace owner.

### Workspace roles

Workspace roles control what a person can do inside one workspace.

#### Workspace owner

Workspace owner is the main owner of one customer workspace. They can:

- manage team members inside that workspace
- update workspace access
- manage workspace settings
- use the modules assigned to them

#### Manager

Manager can run daily operations and manage normal members, but manager is still below workspace owner.

#### Member

Member is the base workspace role. What a member can use depends on which modules and finance permissions are enabled.

## Purchased modules

Modules control which big product areas a user can access.

### Finance

Finance gives access to:

- invoices
- expenses
- approvals
- finance activity
- finance analytics

### Warehouse

Warehouse gives access to:

- products
- low stock alerts
- purchase orders
- warehouse activity

## Finance permissions

Finance permissions are separate from workspace role.

### Viewer

Viewer can read finance information and open read-only finance dashboards.

### Approver

Approver can review and approve or reject finance items that need approval.

### Finance staff

Finance staff can work on day-to-day finance operations such as invoices, expenses, and payment workflows.

### Accountant

Accountant can use accounting-focused views and deeper accounting reporting.

## What customers normally see

Depending on access, a customer may see:

- Inbox
- Analytics / Overview
- Approvals
- Finance
- Warehouse
- Tasks
- Projects
- Workspace settings

If a module or permission is not enabled, that section may be hidden.

## Inbox and bots

The workspace inbox shows active conversations only. That means conversations with real messages appear in the inbox, while empty conversations stay hidden.

If your workspace includes modules, you may also see bots such as:

- FinanceBot
- WareBot

In a real workspace, bots should reflect real backend data, not demo data.

## Analytics in customer language

Analytics is your business snapshot. It helps answer:

- what needs attention today?
- what is overdue?
- what is waiting for approval?
- what is happening in finance?
- what is happening in warehouse?

A simple way to describe Analytics to a customer is:

> Analytics shows the health of your workspace at a glance. It highlights overdue work, approvals, inventory pressure, finance movement, and other items that need action.

## What each analytics area means

### Overview

Overview is the top summary. It shows urgent issues, overdue work, approvals, and overall pressure.

### Finance analytics

Finance analytics usually shows:

- pending approvals
- overdue invoices
- paid amounts
- expense activity
- cash movement

### Warehouse analytics

Warehouse analytics usually shows:

- low stock items
- purchase order progress
- warehouse movement
- stock pressure

### Tasks and projects

These show:

- overdue tasks
- due-today tasks
- project progress
- follow-up work for the team

## Recommended role setup

Here is a simple setup many customers can use.

### Workspace owner

- Workspace role: Owner
- Modules: Finance and Warehouse if both are purchased
- Finance permissions: as needed

### Operations lead

- Workspace role: Manager
- Modules: based on daily responsibilities
- Finance permissions: usually Viewer or Approver

### Finance operator

- Workspace role: Member
- Modules: Finance
- Finance permissions: Finance staff

### External accountant

- Workspace role: Member
- Modules: Finance
- Finance permissions: Accountant

### Warehouse operator

- Workspace role: Member
- Modules: Warehouse

## First steps for a new customer

When a customer receives a workspace, the best first steps are:

1. Confirm the right people are in the workspace.
2. Check who should be Owner, Manager, or Member.
3. Turn on the right modules for each person.
4. Turn on the correct finance permissions for each person.
5. Review inbox, approvals, and analytics.

## Common questions

### Why can’t I see Finance?

Check whether the Finance module is enabled and whether the person has the right finance permission.

### Why can’t I see Warehouse?

Check whether the Warehouse module is enabled.

### Why can I use the workspace but not manage people?

That usually means the user is a Member rather than a Workspace owner.

### Why can the platform owner do more than the workspace owner?

Because platform owner is the app-level administrator. Workspace owner controls only one workspace.

## One-sentence product summary

You can describe the workspace like this:

> This workspace gives your team one place to manage conversations, approvals, finance, warehouse activity, tasks, and business analytics with access controls for each team member.
