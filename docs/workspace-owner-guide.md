# Workspace Owner Guide

Version: 1.0  
Audience: Workspace owners  
Purpose: Help workspace owners manage their own workspace confidently

## What a workspace owner is

Workspace owner is the highest customer-level role inside one workspace.

Workspace owner can:

- manage team members in that workspace
- assign workspace roles
- enable or remove module access
- assign finance permissions
- manage workspace settings
- use the enabled workspace modules

Workspace owner is not the same as platform owner.

## What a workspace owner should control

A workspace owner should usually manage:

- who belongs in the workspace
- who should be manager
- who should stay member
- who gets Finance
- who gets Warehouse
- who gets finance permissions like Viewer or Accountant

## Suggested team structure

Keep the structure simple:

- 1 workspace owner
- 1 or more managers if needed
- members with only the access they need

This makes the workspace easier to understand and safer to manage.

## Adding a new member

When adding a member, decide these things in order:

1. Base workspace role
   - Owner
   - Manager
   - Member
2. Purchased modules
   - Finance
   - Warehouse
3. Finance permissions
   - Viewer
   - Approver
   - Finance staff
   - Accountant

## A practical way to assign access

### Operations manager

- workspace role: Manager
- modules: Finance and Warehouse if they oversee both
- finance permissions: Viewer or Approver

### Finance operator

- workspace role: Member
- modules: Finance
- finance permissions: Finance staff

### Accountant

- workspace role: Member
- modules: Finance
- finance permissions: Accountant

### Warehouse operator

- workspace role: Member
- modules: Warehouse

## Editing team access

When reviewing the member list, check:

- does this person still need access?
- is their role too high?
- do they have unnecessary modules?
- do they have the correct finance permissions?

As a rule:

- give the smallest access that still lets the person do their job
- keep ownership-level access limited

## Owner vs manager

Managers can help run daily operations, but they are still below workspace owner.

Workspace owner should be the final authority for:

- ownership-level changes
- sensitive member-role decisions
- overall workspace structure

## Removing a member

If someone leaves the team or no longer needs access:

1. remove them from the workspace
2. confirm they no longer appear in the active member list
3. confirm their role and access are gone

After removal, they should no longer continue using that workspace.

## Managing modules

Use modules to control broad access:

### Finance module

Give this only to people who need finance-related work.

### Warehouse module

Give this only to people who need warehouse-related work.

If a person does not need a module, keep it off.

## Managing finance permissions

Use finance permissions to define what someone can do inside Finance.

### Viewer

Use for people who only need to see finance information.

### Approver

Use for people who need to approve or reject finance items.

### Finance staff

Use for daily finance operators.

### Accountant

Use for deeper accounting work and accounting-focused reporting.

## Good operating habits

Review your workspace access regularly:

- monthly
- when someone changes teams
- when someone leaves the company
- when a new department starts using the workspace

## Common mistakes to avoid

Avoid:

- giving everyone owner access
- enabling both modules for everyone by default
- giving Accountant access to people who only need Viewer access
- forgetting to remove old members

## Quick checklist for workspace owners

Every time you review the workspace, ask:

1. Are the right people in the workspace?
2. Do they have the right base role?
3. Do they have only the modules they need?
4. Do they have the correct finance permissions?
5. Are there old members who should be removed?

## Short explanation you can tell your team

> The workspace owner manages who is in the workspace, what each person can access, and how the team is set up inside Finance, Warehouse, and the shared workspace tools.
